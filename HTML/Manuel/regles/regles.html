THE VARIOUS MODES OF UNIT CONTROL

Black Moon has two different modes of unit control. The victory of certain missions lies in the control of these two modes; it is, therefore, essential that you master them!

These two modes are the " Fighting " mode and the " Moving " mode. A change in mode can be obtained by pressing the space bar. The mode by default is the " Fighting " mode.

 

FIGHTING MODE :
In the " Fighting " mode, the unit or formation attacks all the enemies it can see. It takes decisions in an autonomous way with the sole intent of destroying its enemies.

In the " Fighting " mode, it is normal for the unit or formation no to do exactly what you expect from it because, although it is attacking the enemy on your orders, it operates in an autonomous manner !

During movement, the unit or formation automatically intercepts all the units it encounters.

In " Fighting " mode, the units focus on the fighting in progress, and cause much more damage! This mode is particularly effective to hold on to a zone, to increase damages inflicted to the enemy and to unleash the power of the enchanters.

 

MOVING MODE :
In " Moving " mode, the unit or formation obeys only the player's orders, and ignores the enemy's attacks sustained during its movements!

During a movement, the unit or formation does not intercept the units it may encounter.

In " Moving " mode, the units focus only on the fastest possible way to carry out the player's orders. Thus guided by the player, they are much quicker in their movements! This mode is particularly effective to carry out a specific action or to get to a site quickly.

 

On the battle map, the " S " key makes it possible to stop time and to give orders.

 

To sum up :

In " Fighting " mode - The units attack in an autonomous way all the enemies it can see.
- The units intercept the enemy during their movements.
- The units provoke much more damage during combat.
In " Moving " mode - The units obey only the player's orders, ignoring the fire of the enemy.
- The units do not intercept the enemy during their movements.
- During movement, the units operate much faster.

 

THE MORALE OF THE UNITS
There are five classes of morale for the fighting units, graded A to E. Morale class A corresponds to units whose morale is unfailing. Morale class E corresponds to units whose morale can fail at any time if the fight turns to its disadvantage.

A section which loses 2/3 of its energy must test its morale. Depending on the morale class, the section is more or less likely to flee the battle. If morale passes the test, the section continues the fighting. If the test fails, an icon appears on your section, which leaves the battle field.

 

FEAR
Certain types of troops that are particularly repulsive or powerful inspire fear in the other fighters who struggle against them. A section fighting against creatures who inspire fear must pass a morale test. If the morale test fails, the section flees the battle field.

 

FLIGHT
A section which runs away from the battle field leaves the map by one of the sides. The sections of retreating soldiers are then lost and irretrievable.

 

REGENERATION
Regeneration is a special aptitude developed by Black Moon for its Trolls. This aptitude provides for the creature to recuperate life points lost during combat. The rhythm for point retrieval is slow. Once it has been killed, the troll no longer benefits from its capacity for regeneration.

 

POISON
Light has the possibility to poison the blades of the fighters' weapons. An enemy struck by a poisoned weapon loses his life points more or less slowly, until the end of the battle. Unfortunately, death often occurs before the battle has ended, unless the victim is cast a care spell.

 

CAVALRY CHARGE
A cavalry section in fighting mode which attacks an enemy by moving towards it is considered to be charging. This section receives a charge bonus which increases its power by 50%.

 

FORMATION BONUS BY TROOP TYPE
A section which maintains its formation receives a 25% bonus for damages, if it leads an attack or protects itself against an opponent.

 

HATRED BETWEEN ELVES AND DWARVES
There has been age-old hatred between the Dwarves and Elves. Dwarves and Elves consider one another mutually as hereditary enemies. The first consequence of this hatred is that, when you become allied to one, the other one unavoidably becomes your enemy. The second consequence is that when Dwarves and Elves fight, they receive an exceptional 20% power bonus.
