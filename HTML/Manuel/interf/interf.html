SKIRMISH

When you select the " Skirmish " option from the menu " New game ", you have access to a window which allows you to choose the armies which will fight and the map on which the battle will take place. This window is divided into three portions :
1 : Select side
2 : Name of regiment
3 : Scroll arrow
4 : Delete regiment
5 : Access to "Casern window"
6 : Sections		 7 : "Skirmish" maps index
8 : Validate
9 : Cancel
10 : Game map
11 : Army positions

The first one corresponds to the regiment controlled by the player. First, you select your side: Light, Black Moon, Justice or Empire (1). The box below gives you the list of regiments available (2). You may scroll the list by using the up and down arrows at the right of the box (3), and delete regiments with the " Delete regiment " icon (4).
The " Casern " button (5) gives you access to the barracks interface. This is the place where you create new regiments or modify them. This interface is itemized in the part " Barracks: Army organization ".
Finally, a table indicates the number of sections contained in the selected regiment (6).

The second portion concerns the choice of battle map. A list of the maps recorded in the " Skirmish " index appears in the upper portion (7). You may scroll it using the up and down arrows on the right-hand side.
Below this list, a preview of the selected map (10) is displayed, on which you may also decide how to dispose your troops on the field. Your troops are represented with a blue square, while your enemies' are represented with a red one. To dispose your troops on the field, click left button on preview, and then click right button to dispose the enemy troops.

The third portion corresponds to a computer-controlled regiment. The principle is the same as for the player.

Lastly, the icon " Validate " (8) starts the game, while the icon " Cancel " (9) returns to the " New game " menu.

 

BARRACKS : ORGANIZE THE ARMY

Access to the barracks is obtained either using the " Casern " button in the " Skirmish " window or, when on campaign, by double-clicking your castle barracks.

In your barracks, you create and organize your regiments. Each regiment is compsed of sections. The regiments are visible on the region map, whereas the sections appear on the battle map.
1 : Information on the regiment capacities
2 : Create regiment
3 : Delete regiment
4 : Top up section
5 : Buy
6 : Sell 		7 : Validate window
8 : Determine quality of troops
9 : Stock of available gold
10 : Scroll arrow
11 : Units available for purchase

The interface of the barracks is divided into three columns :

  • " Regiment " : you may create eight regiments at the most.
  • " Section " : you may create eight sections per regiment at the most.
  • " Reserve " : this column shows you all the units that you may buy (11). At the right of this column, two arrows allow you to scroll the units available (10). Each unit can be found using an icon, its name or its price.

You must first create your regiment. Click " Create regiment " icon (2). A new window shows up. Type in the name of your regiment and validate your selection. Then, organize your sections. To this end, slide the units from the " Reserve " column into the " Section " column. Each time you do this, you must validate your selection in a new window, where you are reminded of unit costs.

The overall sum in gold which you have at your disposal is indicated at the bottom of the " Reserve " column (9).

Under the " Reserve " column, there is a graduated scale (8) which allows you determine the experience level of the units you wish to buy. The more experienced the unit, the higher the price you have to pay for it.

You may also use the icons " Sell " (6) and " Buy " (5) to carry out your transactions instead of sliding the units from one column to the next.

As you form your regiment, informations pop up in the " Regiment " column (1). These indicate what your regiment can do and the places to which it can move, depending on the composition of yours sections.

Finally, the icon " Top up a section " (4) allows you to repair the damage sustained during a battle.
Click on " Top up a section " (4) to buy new units when your regiment has suffered losses.

Click " Validate " (7) to record your selections and quit the barracks.

 

 

REGION MAP
1 : Data bar
2 : Regiment
3 : Particular site
4 : Controls

On the region map, your regiments, as well as those of your enemies, are represented by their banners (2). To move one around, select it and click right button on its destination. Its course is then displayed in the form af arrows.
When you select it, a data bar is displayed at the top of the screen (1).
1 : Plain
2 : River
3 : Forest
4 : Mountain
5 : Fight
 6 : Fortify
7 : Forced march
8 : Break off from fighting
9 : Regiment
10 : Number of action points
This indicates you the number of action points your regiment disposes of (10), as well as the cost of actions and movements according to the type of field.
In the course of its movements, a regiment may cross a particular site marked by an icon or meet an enemy regiment. In both cases, you then have access to the corresponding battle map.
Using the controls at the lower right-hand side of the screen, you can hand over turns and give particular orders to a regiment.
1 : Cancel orders
2 : Go on a forced march
3 : Raise fortifications
4 : Return to castle
5 : End of turn
When you click on the icon " Go on a forced march " (2), the selected regiment wins two action points. Then, you order it to go faster and further. On the other hand, you miss two turns.
The icon " Raise fortifications " (3) allows you to order a selected regiment to prepare a defensive position on the battle map. However, you cannot move while under fortification. If you do, you lose your fortications.
The icon "Cancel orders" (1) cancels the last order given to the selected regiment.
Finally, thanks to the icon " Return to castle " (4), you gain access to resource management, inside your castle.

 

BATTLE MAP
1 : Information on selected section
2 : Information on roll-over section
3 : Spells available
4 : Mini-map
5 : Unit control mode

One or more icons on the top left-hand side of the screen give you various information on the sections selected in the field. For each section the following information is displayed :
1 : Representation of unit
2 : Number of troops
3 : Drop of poison
4 : Cross
5 : Weapon class
6 : Experience level
7 : Button showing the clan's attitude
8 : Morale
9 : Mana
10 : Life

 

  • A representation of the selected section (1)
  • The number of troops in it (2)
  • Its life bar (10) : red-coloured, it informs you about the life points lost and won by your section. The only thing that can restore life points to you is a care spell.
  • Its mana bar (9) : green-coloured, its level decreases every time you cast a spell. Your units win back mana more or less rapidly depending on the type and level of experience.
  • Morale (8) : this is represented by a blue bar. When morale is down to zero, your trooops risk to run away from battle.
  • Weapon class (5)
  • Experience level (6)
  • Drop of poison (3) : its display signifies that at least one unit in the section has been poisoned.
  • Cross (4) : its display signifies that at least one unit in the section is blessed.
  • A button showing the clan's attitude (7). There are four of them :
     
    Player sections
    The player selects his section as he wants to. They obey the player's orders. The player chooses the control mode (moving or fighting mode) of his sections.
     
    Enemy sections
    The enemy sections cannot be controled by the player. They obey the IA's orders. They attack the player's sections and those of any allies of the player.
     
    Neutral sections
    The neutral sections cannot be controled by the player. They obey the IA's orders. They can occasionally attack the player's sections.
     
    Ally sections
    The Ally sections cannot be controled by the player as they have their own command. They do not attack the player's sections but they do attack his enemies. They obey the IA's orders.

An icon on the rop right-hand side of the screen tells your whether the sections selected are in the " Moving " mode or " Fighting " mode . Use the space bar to change the mode of all selected sections.

The mini-map at the lower right-hand side allows you to find your way on the field and to locate enemy troops. Click a spot directly on the mini-map to view it on the main map. You can also order one of your units to go to a site on the mini-map by right-clicking it directly, after selecting your unit.

When you are able to cast spells, their icons appear at the lower left-hand side. In order to use these, select your magician on the field, click the icon of the desired spell and, finally, click the target.

 

 

THE CASTLE : RESOURCE MANAGEMENT
1 : Description
2 : Validate
3 : Price in gold

When you are in your castle, you can prepare your army (see " Barracks ") and improve your troops' skill by buying upgrades. To this end, double-click a building: the window displayed then gives you a list of upgrades which you can purchase in a building, as well as their price in gold (3).
Select any one of them. A descriptive text is displayed at the bottom of the window (1). Click the icon " Validate " (2) in order to buy.

Visit the " Buildings " chapter to find out the list of upgrades available in each building.
